Permutational variable importance for white wine quality dataset¶

About the dataset¶

Wine quality dataset, available at: https://archive.ics.uci.edu/ml/datasets/wine+quality connects a quality score of wine (1-10) with 11 continous attributes of its physiochemical properties:

  • fixed acidity
  • volatile acidity
  • citric acid
  • residual sugar
  • chlorides
  • free sulfur dioxide
  • total sulfur dioxide
  • density
  • pH
  • sulphates
  • alcohol

It contains data on both: red and white wine. I assume that quality predictors would be different for either, so I've arbitraily decided to focus on the white wine.

Used regressors¶

The prediction of wine quality can be interpreted as both: classification and regression task. I chose to treat it as the latter.

Since in previous homeworks Random Forrest seemed to always outperform other models, I've chosen to train multiple RF Regressors, for which I would shuffle the training data (80% of the original puddle). This way I had multiple models with quite similiar MSE on the test dataset. This way, 20 models were trained, of which 5 were selected.

Permutational feature importance¶

To calculate feature importance I've used the metric implemented in scikitlearn.

To make the image more clear I've decided to print out only features of which feature importance was greater than 0.1

As can be seen above, all of the models presented very similiar feature importance scores. In all of the models only the same three displayed features had importance over 0.1, that is alcohol, volatile acidity and free sulfur dioxide.

Unquestionably, alcohol is the most important feature of all. This is consistent with the analysis made throughout previous homeworks.

The consistency of the feature appearance suggest, that those three features are in fact correlated with the quality score in the space of gathered data. This results stand as argument, that the models are in fact observing the real dependencies in the data instead of luckily randomly assigning the score, which in such imbalanced task (most wines scoring 6-7) is a real concern.
