California Housing price regression and LIME¶

The report describes XGBoost and Multi Layer Perceptron learning results on California Housing dataset and decomposition of trained models sample predictions using LIME. The dataset contains 8 features describing blocks of houses. MedHouseVal is an explained variable containing medium house value in $100 000. Below there are quartiles for all of the features:

Below there is a decomposition of selected predictions using LIME with with quartile discretization over features. For the lower price, latitude greater than 3rd quartile had the largest negative impact on the price. Longitude between 1st and 2nd quartile slightly increased the price and most of other features had a small negative impact.

In the medium price case, coordinates influenced the price in the opposite way - latitude increased the price and longitude decreased it. Another important factor was low average number of rooms which decreased the predicted value.

For the largest price, latitude had the greatest positive impact and longitude had quite large negative impact but it was the third factor. The second important feature was high average number of rooms. Median income was also large and influenced the house value positively.

Comparing the selected observations, the geographical coordinates seems to be the most important factor influencing prices of selected blocks of houses. For two lower prices, average number of rooms is low and decreases the price but for the higher price that feature contains high value increasing the median house price.

from matplotlib import pyplot
from lime.lime_tabular import LimeTabularExplainer
from xgboost import XGBRegressor

from sklearn import metrics
from sklearn.model_selection import train_test_split
from sklearn.datasets import fetch_california_housing
from sklearn.preprocessing import MinMaxScaler
from sklearn.pipeline import make_pipeline
from sklearn.neural_network import MLPRegressor

def evaluate(testY, predY):
    pyplot.plot(testY, testY, 'r')
    pyplot.scatter(testY, predY)
    pyplot.title('Median House Value');
    pyplot.xlabel('Actual')
    pyplot.ylabel('Predicted')
    pyplot.show()

    print()
    print("MSE:", metrics.mean_squared_error(testY, predY))
    print("MAE:", metrics.mean_absolute_error(testY, predY))
    print("R2:", metrics.r2_score(testY, predY))

XGBoost Regressor¶

model_XGB = XGBRegressor(objective='reg:squarederror', n_estimators=1000, max_depth=5)
model_XGB.fit(X_train.values, y_train)
